Product summary

1. Private finance clarity in one dashboard

1
Core idea

The app is a private personal finance control center. Its main job is to answer: "How much money can I safely use right now?" after required obligations are reserved.

Version 1 boundary

V1 is manual input only. It does not connect to banks, import broker data, or track investment portfolios. It tracks money moved into investment accounts and manually entered investment profit as income.

App goals

2. Know what is safe to spend

2

Financial confidence

Show total money, committed obligations, and real available money without spreadsheet-style mental math.

Fast daily entry

Make common expense entry possible in under 10 seconds using defaults, recent categories, and quick actions.

Long-term control

Reserve money for annual expenses, debt payoff, credit cards, and fixed budgets before money appears available.

User personas / intended user

3. Designed for one private owner

3

Primary user

A salary-based user in Thailand who receives income around the 25th, uses multiple accounts and credit cards, supports family, pays recurring services, and wants a calm view of usable money.

Usage behavior

Checks dashboard on mobile, records expenses daily, reviews budgets during the month, and updates debts, subscriptions, and sinking funds when plans change.

Core financial rules

4. Obligations are reserved before spending

4

Money is grouped by purpose

  • Real available money uses only cash-like accounts: main bank, other bank, cash, and wallet.
  • Investment accounts are excluded from available cash and shown separately as investment usage or tracking.
  • Only unpaid obligations and unspent reserves reduce real available money.

No double counting

  • Expenses paid by cash or bank already reduced account balances and must not be subtracted again.
  • Transfers between accounts move money only and must not count as expenses.
  • Credit card spending creates liability first, then decreases cash only when the card is paid.

Transaction types

  • income, expense, transfer, credit_card_expense.
  • credit_card_payment, debt_payment, investment_transfer.
  • sinking_fund_reserve for money intentionally reserved for annual costs.

Salary weekend adjustment logic

6. Early payment, same cycle

6
Rule

If the 25th falls on Saturday or Sunday, salary is actually paid on the Friday before the 25th. The app still counts that salary as income for the financial cycle starting on the 25th.

Implementation behavior

  • Store both actual payment date and assigned cycle start date.
  • Dashboard cycle totals use assigned cycle, not only transaction date.
  • Show a small label when salary is "paid early for next cycle."

Credit card billing cycle requirements

15. Card lifecycle separates spending, statements, and payments

15

Card and cycle fields

  • Card name, billing cut date, and payment due date.
  • Current cycle spending before the statement closes.
  • Statement amount due once a billing cycle is issued.
  • Paid amount, remaining payable, and status: unpaid, partial, paid.

Lifecycle behavior

  • A credit card expense increases card liability and current cycle spending.
  • It does not decrease cash-like account balances immediately.
  • A card payment decreases cash and reduces remaining payable.
  • Only the remaining payable should reduce real available money.

Statement structure

Add a credit card statement-cycle structure so each card can track current spending separately from issued statements, due dates, paid amount, and remaining payable.

Investment usage requirements

16. Track money used for investment, not portfolios

16
V1 scope

The app records money moved to an investment account as investment usage. It does not track holdings, market value, unrealized gains, or broker balances.

Cash separation

Investment accounts are excluded from real available money. Moving money to investment is recorded as an investment_transfer, shown separately from normal spending, and not treated as a daily expense.

Profit handling

If investments are sold for profit, the user records the profit manually as side income from stock trading, crypto trading, or other income.

Dashboard display

The dashboard should show investment money used in the current cycle as a separate tracking number, not as part of spendable cash or total net worth.

Dashboard calculation logic

17. Real available money formula

17
Main dashboard formulaCash-like balance = main bank + other bank + cash + walletReal available money = cash-like balance - unpaid obligations - unspent reserved budgets - remaining credit card payable - planned debt payments - monthly sinking fund reservesDo not subtract expenses already paid from cash or bank because those balances already decreased.

Cash vs net worth

Real available money is a cash-availability number, not total net worth. Investment account balances are excluded and shown separately.

Dashboard must show

Cash-like money now, income in current cycle, unpaid obligations, unspent reserved budgets, sinking fund reserves, planned debt, remaining card payable, and real available money.

Daily guidance

Daily available money equals remaining real available money divided by remaining days in the financial cycle, after preserving required reserves.

Cycle snapshots

Show miscellaneous budget remaining, luxury budget remaining, debt remaining, current card cycle spending, statement payable, and investment money used in the current cycle.

Double-counting guardrail

Paid expenses, account transfers, card payments already reflected in cash balances, and investment transfers must not be subtracted again as normal expenses.

Unpaid obligation examples

Upcoming bills, unpaid monthly subscriptions, unpaid statement balances, planned debt payments, and unspent sinking fund reserves.

Supabase RLS and privacy strategy

21. Private by default

21

Access model

  • Supabase Auth login is required for every app screen.
  • No public financial routes or public data endpoints.
  • Signup disabled by default or controlled through allowlist/config.

RLS rules

  • Enable Row Level Security on every user-owned table.
  • All records include user_id linked to auth.uid().
  • Select, insert, update, and delete policies require user_id = auth.uid().
Security acceptance rule

A user must never be able to read, create, update, or delete another user's finance records, even if they manually change request IDs in the browser.

Open questions and assumptions

25. Decisions to confirm before coding

25

Assumptions

  • Default app language is Thai, with English available in Settings.
  • Default currency is THB.
  • Manual input only in v1.
  • One private owner is the primary use case.
  • No investment portfolio tracking in v1.

Open questions

  • Should signup be fully disabled after the first user is created, or controlled by an invite/allowlist?
  • Should account balances be calculated only from transactions, or manually adjustable with reconciliation entries?
  • Should budget overspending reduce real available money immediately or stay visible as a separate warning?
  • Should annual sinking funds reserve money in a virtual bucket or require transfer to a real account?
  • Should credit card "amount due" be manually entered, calculated from card transactions, or support both?
